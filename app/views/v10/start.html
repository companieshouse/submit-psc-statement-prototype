Check identity verification details for a PSC or relevant legal entity

You can use this service to:

  • check when a company's PSCs and RLEs need to provide verification details
  • provide a Companies House personal code for an individual person with significant control (PSC), or relevant officer details for a relevant legal entity (RLE)
  • request a 14 day extension for a PSC or a 28 day extension for an RLE if you need more time

You cannot use this service if the PSC has their personal information protected at Companies House (or is waiting for the results of an application)

When you need to provide verification details

You must wait until the company has reached its confirmation statement date before you can provide verification details for its PSCs or RLEs. You will not be able to do it early.

The deadline for individual PSCs is 14 days after the confirmation statement date, and RLEs have 28 days.

You can see the deadline dates for a company's PSCs and RLEs by signing in to this service.

! Warning If a PSC or RLE does not comply with identity verification requirements, they will be committing an offence and may have to pay a financial penalty or fine. We may also display an annotation against their name on the public register.

How to apply for an extension

You must wait until the company has reached its confirmation statement date before you can request an extension to the deadline for a PSC or RLE.

If your extension request for a PSC is successful the PSC will be given an additional 14 days to provide identity verification details.

If your extension request for a RLE is successful the RLE will be given an additional 28 days to provide iidentity verification details.

You can only request one extension for a PSC or RLE.

What is a Companies House personal code?
This is an 11 character code that is given to a person after they have verified their identity with Companies House.

If you're providing verification details for a relevant officer you'll also need their full name and date of birth.

The relevant officer must be a director of the RLE, or someone whose roles and responsibilities correspond to that of a company director. They must be aged 16 or over.

For more information on PSCs, RLEs and relevant officers, read the guidance on people with significant control.
