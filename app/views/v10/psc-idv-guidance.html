Identity verification for people with significant control (PSCs)

What PSCs need to do to comply with identity verification requirements for Companies House.

What identity verification means for PSCs

If you are a person with significant control (PSC) of a company, you need to verify your identity for Companies House. This is to confirm that you are who you say you are.

When you’ve successfully verified, you’ll be given a Companies House personal code. You must provide this code so that we can link your verified identity to the correct PSC name on our records.

You only need to verify your identity and get a Companies House personal code once.

However, you need to provide your personal code separately for each role you have at a company. For example, for:

  • each role you hold at the same company (for example, if you are both a director and a PSC)
  • any roles you hold at other companies

When and how to verify your identity

You can verify your identity for Companies House at any time.

There are different ways to do this, including:

  • online using GOV.UK One Login
  • in person at the Post Office
  • using a Companies House authorised agent (such as an accountant or solicitor)

For more information on each of these methods, read the guidance on verifying your identity for Companies House.

When you need to provide your Companies House personal code

There’s a specific time period for providing your personal code to link your verified identity to the correct PSC name on our records.

This is different for everyone, and it depends on whether you became a PSC of the company before or after identity verification for Companies House was first introduced on [date].

If you became a PSC before [IDV go live date]

You need to wait until the company’s confirmation statement date. You will not be able to provide your personal code before this date.

You will then have 14 days from the confirmation statement date to provide your personal code.

If you became a PSC after [IDV go live date]

You’ll need to provide your personal code within 14 days of the date Companies House accepted your PSC notification filing.

You can see the deadline dates for a company’s PSCs by signing in to the Provide verification details for a PSC service.

! Warning If a PSC does not comply with identity verification requirements, they will be committing an offence and may have to pay a financial penalty or fine. We may also display an annotation against their name on the public register.
